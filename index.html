Rabi Oscillations Simulation

How does the spin of an electron-like particle with:
  • mass m = electron mass
  • charge q = e
  • initial state (a + bi, c + di) = ( + i , + i )
oscillate in a magnetic field with B = x 10^(-5) G, B0 = x 10^(-5) G, and omega = rad/s ?

About the visualization

The two plots above shows how the probabilities of the particle being in the spin-up state and in the spin-down state vary with time.

The left plot graphs probability over time: the upper curve shows the probability of the particle being in the spin-up state and is bounded below by the (upside-down) curve of the the probability of being in the spin-down state. The height of the bounded area is always 1, since there is a probability of 1 that the particle will be in either one of the two states at any given time.

The plot on the right is a visualization of the spin at any given time, over the same time frame as the graph on the left. The two arrows represent the probability of the particle being in the two states, with a longer arrow meaning greater probability. If you see one long arrow pointing up and no arrow pointing down, the particle is with 100 % certainty in the spin-up state.

For any given choice of the magnetic field strengths, it is possible to obtain a 100 % chance of the particle being in the spin-down state -- even if it is intially in the spin-up state! Can you find that frequency?
